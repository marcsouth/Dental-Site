Preventative Dentistry

Prevention of dental diseases is certainly more desirable than treating and correcting a problem. The risks of dental decay and gum disease are minimized by controlling the plaque and tartar on your teeth. Even more important than the thorough in-office cleaning provided by our dentist and hygienists is your daily routine referred to as home care. We will make sure to explain the techniques and implements specific to your needs. Tooth decay risk is also minimized by proper brushing and flossing, and minimizing sugar in the diet, especially between meal sticky snacks or frequent sweet drinks. Fluoride toothpaste and stronger prescription pastes may be recommended. Remember: ONLY YOU CAN PREVENT TOOTH DECAY!” Young children begin preventive visits as soon as all first teeth are in, usually by 3 years of age. We love providing dental care to children!

Did You Know...

In a lifetime, the average American spends approximately 38.5 total days just brushing his or her teeth.

Restorative Dentistry

Restorative dentistry includes fillings to restore decayed teeth, and also crowns. In our practice, a variety of materials are employed, and your procedure will be painless thanks to expert application of topical and local anesthetics delivered by a caring professional, Dr. Crepon. A crown, also known as a cap may be recommended if your tooth is mostly filling in order to preserve it and minimize the chance of breakage in the future. Did You Know 80 percent of the tooth decay in our nation occurs in just 20 percent of the population. Contrary to popular belief, this decay is not caused by “soft teeth”, but rather a lack of brushing and flossing with excessive sugary snacks and drinks. Most dental disease is preventable. YOUR TEETH SHOULD LAST A LIFETIME!

Did You Know...

Each American is known to have at least three fillings in the past year.

Cosmetic Dentistry

Your smile depends on the straight alignment of teeth with a naturally white shade. Often the wisest way to improve your tooth shade is to consider our home whitening treatment , a non-invasive affordable path to whiter teeth. This treatment utilizes custom-made trays which deliver a whitening gel to your teeth at home for 30 minute sessions usually totaling about 12 in number. The straightening of your teeth’ s alignment may be best accomplished orthodontically using braces or the Invisalign system of very popular treatment with clear guards instead of traditional braces. We refer our patients to an orthodontist for these treatments. Cosmetic dental treatment may also include composite bonding as well as veneers and crowns.

Did You Know...

Single crown is the most common restorative procedure. Approx 2.3 million implant-supported crowns are made annually.

Prosthetic Dentistry

Prosthetic dentistry involves the replacement of missing teeth. Our patients are made aware of all of their choices in this regard. These include implants, fixed bridgework and removable dentures, both partial and full. Dr. Crepon has extensive experience and knowledge with respect to all of these treatment modalities. All employ the finest laboratories and materials.

Did You Know...

Implants function like natural teeth, transferring the energy of biting and chewing into the jawbone beneath the newly created tooth.

Pediatric Dentistry

Dr. Crepon simply loves to work with children. From instructing them about excellent dental health practices to restoring a cavity if need be, children as young as 2 or 3 years old are welcomed into our practice. Tips about brushing, flossing and healthy eating habits are presented with enthusiasm and humor.

Did You Know...

About 20 percent of kids between the ages of 5 and 11 have at least 1 untreated decayed tooth.
